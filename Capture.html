Capturing 2-D Facial Profile for The Continuous Profile®

 


Designing a new Continuous Profile® is simply a matter of extracting the salient features of the human head in profile from a photo or scanned image. Minor editing of the image, including alignment and resizing may be required to establish the final tool path. The capture process starts with a digital image of the object using a digital camera capable of creating industry standard file formats such as JPEG or PNG.


Following are considerations when taking the digital image of the facial profile used to create the 3D rendering of The Continuous Profile®. Note that the entire image capture step is easy and should not take more than several minutes.


You will need:


  1. •Subject (Person) to be photographed. Family pets may also be candidates, but good luck getting Muffy to pose.


  3. •Digital camera or cell phone camera. Flash turned off to avoid shadows.


  5. •Background (wall or door) of uniform color (preferably bright white) that   is different from the person being photographed.


  7. •Working knowledge of Photo editing software, such as PhotoShop or PaintShop Pro.


Considerations for capturing the 2D image of the person’s profile include:


  1. 1.The Subject should not wear eyeglasses or jewelry that extends beyond their natural profile


  3. 2.Hair can cause distortions in the resulting 3D rendering and should be combed back unless it is normally styled close to the head. Try to avoid wispy hair extending beyond the front portion of the facial profile


  5. 3.Mouth can be either gently closed or opened, as in a smile. Try to avoid tightly closing lips, which will lose detail of the profile

  6. 4.If possible, clothing should not be high on the neck since it may be picked up as part of the profile.


  8. 5.The camera should be at least 5 feet away from the subject and the subject should be at least 2 feet from the background. Make sure there are no shadows cast on the background from either the subject or other source


  10. 6.The subject’s head should be perpendicular to the camera to capture as much profile information as possible
